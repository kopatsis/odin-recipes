Pickled Pig's Feet

A vintage recipe for a traditional American treat. Pig's feet can be specially ordered from most butcher shops. Don't knock it till you try it!

From justapinch.com

Ingredients:

  • 4 good-sized boiled pigs feet with uppers
  • 1 quart vinegar
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoons whole cloves
  • 1 tablespoons broken cinnamon sticks
  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 2 teaspoons pepper
  • 1/2 large onion, cut into eights
  • 1 blade of mace

Instructions:

  1. Clean feet carefully and cover with hot water. Simmer until meat will separate from bones, then remove carefully with skimmer.
  2. Place in stone jar, taking out the largest bones. Save the water for later use and let cool.
  3. Heat vinegar with bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon, salt, pepper, onion, and mace. Simmer slowly for 45 minutes but do not boil at any time.
  4. Remove cake of fat from top of saved cooking water.
  5. Add about 1 quart of the water to vinegar.
  6. Strain liquid through a sieve and pour over meat in jar. Chill 2 days.
